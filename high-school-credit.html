North Star Award

The North Star Award is the top-section award for Beaver Scouts. It is typically completeled in the 3rd year of Beavers and is considered the capstone of your time with the colony. There are a variety of requirements to achieve the North Star Award, including talking with your colony Scouter about your progression as a Beaver Scout, completing at least 5 stages of OAS (Outdoor Adventure Skills) and completing at least 5 hours of community service. The final requirement to recieve the award is to complete a meaningful service project, which is completed as a group in the final year of Beaver Scouts.


For more information about the award, click on the link at the bottom of the page.


Earning the North Star Award will give you an epic badge. Wear it on your Cub uniform to show your achievement in Beavers!

Seeonee Award

The Seeonee Award is the top-section award for Cub Scouts. It is typically completed in the 3rd year of Cubs and marks your achievement in your time with the pack. There are a variety of requirements to achieve the Seeonee Award, including a discussion about your progression as a Cub Scout, completing at least 10 stages of OAS (Outdoor Adventure Skills) and completing at least 15 hours of community service. The final requirement is to complete a meaningful service project, which is typically completed in the final year of Cub Scouts and can be done individually or as a group.


For more information about the award, click on the link at the bottom of the page.


Earning the Seeonee Award will give you another epic badge. You can wear this on your Scout uniform along with your North Star Award to show your achievement so far as a Beaver and Cub Scout.

Chief Scout Award

The Chief Scout Award is the top-section award for Scouts. It is typically completed in the 4th year of Scouts and marks your achievement in your time with the troop. There are a variety of requirements to achieve the Chief Scout Award, including a discussion about your progression as a Scout, completing at least 18 stages of OAS (Outdoor Adventure Skills) and completing at least 30 hours of community service. The final requirement is to complete a meaningful service project, which is typically completed in the final year of Scouts and is usually done individually, but can be done with a group.


For more information about the award, click on the link on the bottom of the page.


Earning the Chief Scout Award will not only give you an epic badge, but it can count as a High School Credit towards the number of credits needed to graduate from High School. You can wear the badge on your Venturer uniform along with the Seeonee Award and the North Star Award to show your achievement.
